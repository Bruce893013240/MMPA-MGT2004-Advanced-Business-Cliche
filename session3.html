Competitive Strategy: Offense, Defense, Scope & Value Creation
竞争战略：进攻、防御、经营范围与价值创造
3.1 Strategic Offense 进攻战略
  • Apply your strongest resources where rivals are most vulnerable — don't attack where they're strongest 用你最强的资源打击竞争对手最薄弱的地方——不要硬碰对手的强项
  • Element of surprise — unexpected moves can be more effective 出其不意往往比正面进攻更有效
  • Swift, decisive, overwhelming action — have capacity to follow through 行动要快、要决断，甚至要让对手不知所措
  • Game theory mindset — always assume competitors are also planning; anticipate counter-responses 博弈论思维——你在做计划，竞争对手也在做，要预测他们的反应

Principal Offensive Options

OptionDescription
Better product at lower priceTip the price/benefit balance in your favor 以更低价格提供同等或更好的产品
Leapfrog with next-gen productJump over where competitors are today with an innovation 推出跨越式的下一代产品
Disruptive innovationFundamentally change the value proposition 颠覆性创新，彻底改变价值主张
Hit-and-run guerrillaIncremental market share gains over time 游击式营销，逐步积累市场份额
Preemptive strikeMove fast when limited resources or rare opportunity exists with short shelf life 在稀缺资源或稀有机会出现时先发制人

进攻战略的核心是找到竞争对手最薄弱的地方，用你最强的资源去打。要有博弈论（game theory）思维——你在做计划，竞争对手也在做，要预测他们的反应。进攻策略包括：推出跨越式下一代产品、颠覆性创新、游击式营销、以及在稀缺资源或稀有机会出现时先发制人。

3.2 Defensive Strategies 防御战略

Two main approaches: ① Block challengers from entering; ② Signal strong retaliation.

Lock customers inMulti-year contracts, loyalty programs, switching costs 通过长期合同锁定客户
Product announcement signalingApple effect — market waits for announced products, delaying rivals 宣布新产品让市场等待，拖延竞争对手
Price-matching commitmentPublicly commit to match any competitor's price — removes price as a weapon 公开承诺跟进任何竞争对手的价格
Cash reserves as deterrentAbundant cash signals credible ability to retaliate aggressively 充足现金储备向竞争对手传递强烈报复信号

防御策略有两大思路：一是主动堵住竞争对手进来的通道，二是发出强烈报复信号吓退竞争对手。苹果提前宣布产品让市场等待是很好的防御策略——消费者等着买苹果产品，竞争对手就很难在这段时间抢占市场。

3.3 Timing: First Mover vs. Late Mover 先发与后发

There is no universal answer. Both have merits depending on conditions.

Creating switching costs before rivals arrive 创造切换成本Leapfrog with superior technology if first product was primitive 用更好的技术超越早期进入者

先发制人和后发制人各有优劣，关键在于情境。先发优势在于品牌声誉、专利保护、学习曲线和网络效应（尤其在数字平台）。但先发也有风险：高昂的开拓成本、被后来者用更好的技术超越。苹果买断触摸屏玻璃供应，有效阻止了黑莓进入平板市场，是一个「阻止先发优势」的现实案例。

Burberry — Forward Integration

Japan franchise positioned Burberry as everyday apparel vs. luxury globally. As travel increased, brand inconsistency became visible. Burberry took control of all distribution channels to protect brand equity.

Key disadvantages of VI: Reduced flexibility to adopt new technology; capacity matching problems; new capabilities required; volume risk.

垂直整合向上游整合（向供应商）和向下游整合（向客户/销售渠道）。博柏利（Burberry）是前向整合的好例子——因为日本特许经营商把品牌定位成日常服饰，与全球奢侈品定位不符，博柏利不得不收回所有销售渠道控制权。垂直整合的最大风险是：资本承诺大、技术更新被拖慢、容量匹配问题复杂。

3.6 Outsourcing & Strategic Alliances 外包与战略联盟

When Outsourcing Makes Sense 外包的适用条件

  • Activity can be done better and cheaper by a specialist AND is NOT critical to competitive advantage 外部专家能做得更好更便宜，且该活动不是核心竞争力
  • Flexibility needed — avoids locking in capital; allows quick switching of suppliers 需要灵活性时——避免资本锁定，方便快速切换供应商
  • Focus internal resources on true core competencies that differentiate the firm 把内部资源集中在真正创造差异化价值的核心能力上
Risk: Over-outsourcing leads to loss of key capabilities and loss of control over your own destiny.

Key risk of alliances: proprietary technology leakage — partners learn core IP and then become direct competitors. This has happened many times.

战略联盟是介于外包和并购之间的方式，兼具灵活性和资源共享的优点。一些大公司同时维持200多个联盟。最大的风险是专有技术泄露——有很多先例，盟友学会了你的核心技术或流程后，转身成为你的主要竞争对手。

3.7 Eliminating Strategic Overload 消除战略超载

The problem: too many objectives, strategies, metrics, and activities. A strategic initiative is only worthwhile if it does one or more of:

  • Increases customer willingness to pay (WTP) — creates genuine value customers will pay more for 提升客户的支付意愿（WTP）
  • Increases employee willingness to work / sell — makes the firm more attractive to needed talent 提升员工的工作意愿——让公司对所需人才更有吸引力
  • Increases supplier willingness to sell — creates better terms, quality, or reliability from suppliers 提升供应商的出售意愿——获得更好的条款、质量或可靠性
Focus on value, not profit. Profit is the OUTCOME of creating value — not the strategy itself.

战略超载问题：很多组织设置了太多目标和战略，结果什么都做不好。一个战略举措只有在能提升客户支付意愿、员工工作意愿，或供应商出售意愿时才值得做。利润是创造价值的结果，不是目标本身。

3.8 Value Maps & Complements 价值地图与补充品

Value Map: a diagnostic tool. Steps: (1) customer research by segment, (2) identify highest-priority attributes, (3) plot your performance vs. competitors.

  • Invest in a SMALL number of attributes — don't spread resources across everything 只投资少数几个关键属性，不要把资源分散到所有维度上
  • Don't play catch-up — resist investing in areas where you're weak; competitors may be unbeatable there 不要追赶竞争对手的强项——他们可能永远无法被超越
  • Double down on your strengths — extend your lead and widen the differentiation gap 继续加大在你已经领先的地方的投入，拉大与竞争对手的差距
Apple — Complements Strategy

Apple added iPod, iPad, App Store, iTunes — making the core hardware dramatically more valuable. The ecosystem raises WTP for everything. 'Profit pools' shift over time — track where margin is moving and defend high-margin positions.

价值地图：你先通过市场调研找出对某个客户细分群体最重要的属性，然后把你自己和竞争对手在这些属性上的表现画出来。文章的建议是：不要去追赶竞争对手的强项，而是继续加大在你已经领先的地方的投入。一旦客户觉得你和对手差不多，他们就只看价格了——这是最糟糕的竞争处境。补充品（如苹果的App Store）能让核心产品更有价值，形成良性循环。

3.9 Case Study: Intel Corporation 案例精读：英特尔公司
Intel Corporation: Outsourcing Dilemma  ·  英特尔：外包困境

Ivey Publishing W25607 · Bhattacharya & Agnihotri, 2022

Opening Situation  /  开篇情境

In January 2021, the newly appointed chief executive officer (CEO) of California-based Intel Corporation (Intel), Patrick P. Gelsinger, needed to decide which of three actions to take: outsource advanced chip production to a competitor, like Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company Ltd. (TSMC); completely spin off Intel foundries, like Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (AMD) had done before becoming a leading chip design company; or outsource some production to fill gaps in its product line as it addressed manufacturing issues.

2021年1月，英特尔新上任的CEO帕特·盖尔辛格面临三个选择：把先进芯片的生产外包给竞争对手（比如台积电TSMC）；像AMD当年那样彻底剥离自己的晶圆厂，专注芯片设计；或者在解决自身制造问题的同时，把部分产品线外包出去补缺口。

Intel, a vertically integrated semiconductor company, had been experiencing delays in producing 10 nanometre (nm) and 7 nm chips. The delays were adversely impacting both its reputation and its customers.

英特尔是一家垂直整合的半导体公司，但在10纳米和7纳米芯片的生产上一直在拖期，这严重损害了公司的声誉，客户也深受其苦。

In recent years, Intel, a semiconductor computer circuit leader, had lost its position as the industry's undisputed leader as competition from new rivals such as NVIDIA Corporation (NVIDIA) and AMD increased, and as it experienced delays in the manufacturing of next-generation chips.

曾经的行业老大英特尔，近年来因为英伟达和AMD的强势崛起，加上自己在下一代芯片制造上频频延误，老大地位早就坐不稳了。

In July 2020, Intel considered outsourcing chip manufacturing to TSMC, a leading contract manufacturer located in Taiwan. The news that Intel was considering such an outsourcing decision led its stock price to plummet by 16 per cent.

2020年7月，英特尔考虑把芯片制造外包给台湾的台积电。这个消息一出，英特尔股价立刻暴跌16%。

Intel chips were highly customized, and according to experts, using any standardization process of manufacturing would decrease chip performance. Moreover, due to geopolitical tensions between the United States and China, the US government was also not supportive of outsourcing critical chip manufacturing to Taiwan, a country that had tense relations with China.

英特尔的芯片高度定制化，专家认为一旦走标准化生产流程，芯片性能就会打折扣。再加上中美地缘政治紧张，美国政府也不太愿意看到核心芯片制造被外包到与中国关系微妙的台湾。

Experts believed that the delay in Intel's chipset manufacturing would speed up the growth of competitors like AMD and could even give them the opportunity to dominate the personal computer (PC) processor market. Between 2019 and 2020, Intel's shares grew by nearly 26 per cent, while AMD and NVIDIA reported share price growth of approximately 398 per cent and 300 per cent, respectively.

专家认为英特尔的制造延误会给AMD们提供弯道超车的机会，甚至有可能让对手拿下PC处理器市场的主导权。2019到2020年间，英特尔股价涨了约26%，而AMD和英伟达同期涨幅分别高达398%和300%，差距一目了然。

Intel investors and customers were expecting Intel's newly appointed CEO, Gelsinger, to address Intel's manufacturing issues immediately. Wayne Lam, senior director of research at CCS Insight, said, "They [Intel] need to figure out whether they can get their technology back on track, or just give up and do what everybody else does and buy from TSMC or other foundries."

投资者和客户都眼巴巴地等着盖尔辛格出手解决制造问题。CCS Insight的研究总监韦恩·林说："英特尔现在必须想清楚，到底能不能把技术拉回正轨，还是干脆认了，跟其他人一样去台积电买货。"

Should Gelsinger outsource the manufacturing of chips to a contract manufacturer like TSMC? In the long run, should Intel remain a vertically integrated unit, or should it spin off its manufacturing division?

盖尔辛格到底该不该把芯片制造外包给台积电这样的代工厂？从长远看，英特尔是该继续保持垂直整合，还是该把制造部门剥离出去单干？

Background  /  背景

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, semiconductor pioneers, founded Intel in 1968. In January 2021, Intel announced Gelsinger as the new CEO, who would take charge from February 2021. Gelsinger had previously worked for VMware, Inc., a client of Intel, where he had been CEO since 2012 and had transformed the company into a recognized global leader in cloud infrastructure, almost tripling the company's annual revenues.

半导体先驱罗伯特·诺伊斯和戈登·摩尔于1968年共同创立了英特尔。2021年1月，英特尔宣布盖尔辛格出任新CEO，2月正式上任。他此前在英特尔的客户VMware担任CEO，将那家公司打造成了全球云基础设施的领军企业，年收入几乎翻了三倍。

Out of his 40-year technology career, Gelsinger had worked with Intel for a total of 30 years, including as its first chief technology officer in 2001. He was also the architect of the original 80486 processor, had led 14 microprocessor programs, and had held critical roles in Intel's Core and Xeon families of chip development.

盖尔辛格在科技行业干了40年，其中30年都在英特尔，还做过英特尔首任首席技术官（2001年）。他是80486处理器的最初架构师，主导过14款微处理器项目，在Intel Core和Xeon系列芯片研发中都扮演过关键角色。

However, Gelsinger was taking over Intel at a time when the company was experiencing stagnant growth in net income (2018–2020). Research and development expenses were also stagnant. In 2020, Intel had more than 110,000 employees.

不过，盖尔辛格接手的是一个净利润增长停滞（2018–2020年）、研发支出也毫无起色的英特尔。2020年，英特尔员工总数超过11万人。

Shifts in the Semiconductor Industry  /  半导体行业的变迁

The cost of setting up chip factories or foundries was several billions of dollars and involved machines that could imprint circuits only several atoms wide. The chip industry continually shifted to smaller circuitry, requiring new factory equipment that could produce it.

建一座芯片工厂或晶圆厂动辄要花几十亿美元，还需要用到能在几个原子宽度上刻印电路的精密设备。芯片行业一直在朝更小制程迈进，这就意味着要不断购置新设备。

For example, in 2015, 14 nm chips were popular, but by 2018, the industry had moved toward 10 nm chips, requiring new equipment set-up. In 2019, AMD and Apple, Inc. (Apple) introduced 7 nm chips to the world. The nanometre size referred to the line width between transistors on a chip. A smaller number implied more cutting-edge chips, which were more challenging and expensive to develop.

比如2015年流行14纳米，到2018年行业就转向10纳米了，设备得重新置换。2019年AMD和苹果相继推出7纳米芯片。所谓纳米数，指的是芯片上晶体管之间的线宽，数字越小，芯片越先进，研发难度和成本也越高。

TSMC was the leading manufacturer of semiconductor chips. As the cost of operating manufacturing units, developing new process technologies, and purchasing new manufacturing equipment increased, most semiconductor companies outsourced manufacturing to companies like TSMC and turned their focus to chip design.

台积电是全球领先的半导体代工厂。随着运营制造设施、开发新工艺、购置新设备的成本越来越高，大多数半导体公司都选择把制造外包给台积电这样的代工厂，自己专心搞芯片设计。

For chip designing, Intel faced stiff competition from AMD and NVIDIA. AMD desktop processors were faster than Intel's desktop processors, and AMD was equally competitive as Intel in terms of data. AMD founder Jerry Sanders, commenting on in-house foundries, said, "Real men have fabs [fabrication plants]."

在芯片设计这块，英特尔面对AMD和英伟达的激烈竞争。AMD的桌面处理器性能比英特尔更快，在数据处理方面也不遑多让。AMD创始人杰里·桑德斯有句名言："真男人才有自己的晶圆厂。"

Eventually, AMD spun off its fabs due to financial struggles and relied on third-party foundries for manufacturing. AMD continued designing chips in house and outsourced manufacturing of its 7 nm chips to TSMC. By doing so, AMD saved the billions of dollars it would have spent to establish expensive manufacturing plants in the United States. AMD 7 nm chips were lauded, and experts believed this would further widen AMD's competitive edge after the launch of 5 nm chips in 2022.

但AMD最终因为财务压力不得不剥离工厂，转向第三方代工。AMD把7纳米芯片的生产外包给台积电，自己专注设计，省下了在美国建厂的几十亿美元。AMD的7纳米芯片获得广泛好评，专家认为2022年5纳米芯片推出后，AMD的竞争优势还会进一步拉大。

In terms of gross margin, chip designing had a higher gross margin than manufacturing; for example, NVIDIA had gross margins of between 60 and 65 per cent, while TSMC had a 50 per cent gross margin. In 2020, NVIDIA, despite being a much smaller company in revenue and profits, was valued higher than Intel due to its better growth potential.

从毛利率来看，芯片设计比制造更赚钱：英伟达毛利率高达60–65%，台积电也有50%左右。2020年，英伟达的营收和利润体量远不如英特尔，市值却超过了英特尔，靠的就是更被看好的增长潜力。

Intel's In-House Manufacturing  /  英特尔的自主制造

When most US chip companies sold or shut their domestic plants and outsourced component manufacturing to Asia, Intel kept manufacturing in house, believing this would help it improve operations and create better semiconductors. Intel had 15 wafer fabs worldwide at ten locations. Intel spent several billions of dollars to update its factories and considered these facilities a critical advantage that in the past had kept it ahead of the rest of the industry.

当大多数美国芯片公司纷纷卖厂、关厂，把制造转移到亚洲的时候，英特尔坚持自己做，认为这样才能持续优化运营、做出更好的芯片。英特尔在全球十个地点共有15座晶圆厂，在工厂升级上投入了数十亿美元，并将这些制造设施视为保持行业领先的核心竞争优势。

Moreover, Intel believed in the "copy exactly" strategy. Under this strategy, researchers spent years perfecting a new manufacturing technique. Once satisfied, Intel engineers worked meticulously to ensure that factories followed the same technology and production process worldwide.

英特尔还奉行"完全复制"战略——研究人员花好几年把某项新工艺打磨到极致，然后工程师们严格确保全球各工厂都采用完全相同的技术和生产流程，一点不走样。

As Intel's core strategy was to minimize the size of chips, it believed that chip engineers needed to work directly with manufacturing engineers, which would be harder to do with an outside manufacturer.

由于缩小芯片尺寸是英特尔的核心战略，它认为芯片工程师必须和制造工程师紧密协作，这一点如果交给外部代工厂会非常难实现。

Intel also offered foundry services to other semiconductor companies. However, its process, tools, and even terminology differed significantly from general practices adopted by semiconductor manufacturers. Despite being cost-effective, Intel's unique manufacturing process became an obstacle to offering manufacturing services to other semiconductor companies. Consequently, its foundry business was not a success.

英特尔也曾向其他半导体公司提供代工服务，但它的工艺、工具甚至术语体系都和行业通行标准差异很大。虽然成本上有优势，但这套独特的制造体系成了对外提供代工服务的障碍，导致英特尔的代工业务始终没做起来。

It was also trying to develop chip-making capabilities for drones and artificial intelligence (AI) by acquiring deep learning start-up Nervana Systems (in 2016); autonomous vehicle expert Mobileye (in 2017); and drone chip specialist Movidius (in 2016). Intel's competitors, such as AMD and NVIDIA, were also active in designing chips for these product categories. As the largest chip producer globally, Intel attracted the most experienced engineers and scientists, but it had yet to prove that its chip designs for emerging technologies associated with drones, autonomous driving, etc. were superior to those of NVIDIA and AMD.

英特尔还通过并购布局无人机和AI芯片：2016年收购深度学习初创公司Nervana Systems和无人机芯片专家Movidius，2017年收购自动驾驶专家Mobileye。AMD和英伟达也在这些新兴领域积极布局。作为全球最大的芯片制造商，英特尔吸引了最顶尖的工程师和科学家，但在无人机、自动驾驶等新兴技术的芯片设计上，英特尔还没证明自己能打过英伟达和AMD。

Manufacturing Delays & Outsourcing Consideration  /  制造延误与外包考量

As Intel engineers believed in Moore's Law, they were trying to pack 2.7 times more transistors into the same size space as before to avoid having to purchase new production equipment. This, according to Mike Mayberry, Intel's chief technology officer, was a risky leap and required novel production techniques.

英特尔的工程师信奉摩尔定律，想在不换新设备的前提下，在同样面积上塞进2.7倍的晶体管。英特尔首席技术官迈克·梅伯里承认，这是一次冒险的跨越，需要全新的生产技术。

Further, leaders of chip manufacturing at Intel shared very little data on problems with top management. According to Robert Swan, Intel's CEO from January 2019 to January 2021, this slowed the search for solutions and delayed production with new manufacturing techniques. Problems arose as a result, with Intel experiencing repeated manufacturing snags and product shipment delays globally for all clients and customers. TSMC, on the other hand, was regularly sharing data about its manufacturing technology with external designers and often incorporated their suggestions.

更糟的是，英特尔制造端的管理层极少向高层汇报问题。据CEO斯旺说，这导致问题迟迟找不到解决方案，新工艺的量产一再推迟。结果就是英特尔在全球范围内接连出现制造故障和发货延误。反观台积电，则会定期把制造技术数据分享给外部设计方，并经常吸纳他们的建议。

Overcoming production problems was further complicated by the tight linkage between Intel's chip design and its manufacturing operations. Intel optimized designs for its own chip-making machines; thus it could not quickly turn to outside manufacturers for any temporary help. However, since 2019, Intel had begun to increase its manufacturing flexibility using a modular design approach to take advantage of any manufacturing process, whether internal or external.

制造问题之所以难解，还因为英特尔的芯片设计和制造工艺深度绑定——设计就是为自家设备量身定制的，一时半会儿没法找外部代工顶上。不过从2019年开始，英特尔已经在尝试模块化设计，让内外部制造工艺都能灵活适配。

Swan believed that the core issue with Intel was its culture: employees were hiding issues from senior management and not confronting the issues more openly. According to Swan, another issue was that employees believed that Intel had a monopoly on computer chips. When markets for smartphones and other opportunities opened, Intel was not willing to listen to customer needs.

斯旺认为英特尔的核心问题出在文化上：员工习惯对高层隐瞒问题，不愿直面矛盾。另一个问题是员工骨子里觉得英特尔在芯片领域是垄断者，所以当智能手机等新市场兴起时，英特尔根本懒得听客户需求。

As a consequence of the efforts made under Swan's leadership, the chip design process at Intel became up to three times faster. Jim Keller, a senior vice-president at Intel leading the chip design efforts, commented on the design and manufacturing collaboration: "They tell us what we need to know, and we tell them what they need to know," and added that this was "a big transformation."

改革成效显著，芯片设计周期提速了多达三倍。主导芯片设计的高级副总裁吉姆·凯勒这样形容设计与制造的协作："他们把我们需要知道的告诉我们，我们也把他们需要知道的告诉他们。"他说这是"一次巨大的转变"。

In July 2020, Intel was considering outsourcing to avoid production delays. The production yield of its 7 nm process technology was one year behind the original schedule, as quality defects were arising in the manufacturing process. If Intel outsourced chip manufacturing, it would be to TSMC. According to Bank of America analysts, any manufacturing fixes could produce results by late 2022 or 2023 at the earliest.

2020年7月，英特尔为了避免继续延误，开始认真考虑外包。7纳米制程的良率已经比计划落后了整整一年，制造过程中质量缺陷不断出现。如果要外包，对象只会是台积电。美国银行的分析师认为，就算部分外包给台积电，最早也得到2022年底或2023年才能看到成效。

Chris Caso, of Raymond James Financial, Inc. believed that integrated manufacturing was the key competitive edge for Intel. Caso said, "By outsourcing leading edge technology, presumably to TSMC, Intel would give up what has been its main source of competitive advantage for 50 years."

雷蒙德·詹姆斯金融公司的克里斯·卡索则认为，垂直整合制造才是英特尔最核心的竞争优势。他说："把前沿工艺外包出去，英特尔就等于放弃了50年来赖以为傲的最核心竞争优势。"

Outsourcing chip manufacturing to Taiwan also presented geopolitical risks as well as the risk of severe earthquakes, which could delay shipments. TSMC was based in Hsinchu, one of the Taiwanese cities closest to China. China did not recognize Taiwan as an independent country but considered it a breakaway province. In 2020, lawmakers also proposed US$25 billion in funding and tax credits to boost chip production within the United States.

把芯片制造外包到台湾还面临地缘政治风险以及地震风险。台积电总部在新竹，是台湾距离中国大陆最近的城市之一。中国不承认台湾是独立国家，视其为分裂省份。2020年，美国国会议员提议拨款250亿美元并提供税收优惠，以扶持国内芯片生产。

Susquehanna International Group LLP analyst Chris Rolland said, "With the latest push out of process technology, we believe that Intel has zero-to-no chance of catching or surpassing TSMC at least for the next half decade, if not ever."

悉尼国际集团分析师克里斯·罗兰评论道："考虑到最新的工艺进展，我们认为英特尔在未来至少五年内追上或超越台积电的可能性几乎为零，甚至可能永远都不行。"

TSMC's Advantage  /  台积电的优势

GlobalFoundries Inc., Samsung Foundry, and TSMC were pure-play foundries. Their business model involved only manufacturing silicon for other companies rather than designing or launching products they themselves sold. TSMC, Intel, and Samsung Electronics were the only key players competing to further push even smaller nanometre technology. Within TSMC, there was a long-held doctrine that it should "never underestimate Intel."

格芯、三星代工部门和台积电都是纯代工厂，商业模式就是专门帮别人制造芯片，不自己设计和销售产品。台积电、英特尔和三星是仅有的三家有能力继续推进更小制程的关键玩家。台积电内部长期流传着一条信条："永远不要低估英特尔。"

In July 2020, TSMC was mass-producing 5 nm chips for upcoming 5G iPhones. In contrast, Intel's fastest chip relied on 7 nm technology. After Intel announced a delay in launching its 7 nm chips to 2023, TSMC shares rose by 10 per cent. TSMC became more valued than Tesla Inc., Intel, and Procter & Gamble Co. in terms of market capitalization. The contract manufacturer even declared that its next-generation 3 nm chips would be mass-produced by the second half of 2022. Where the production of chips was in the billions per year for TSMC, it was in the millions for Intel.

2020年7月，台积电已在为即将上市的5G iPhone量产5纳米芯片，而英特尔最快的芯片还停留在7纳米。英特尔宣布7纳米芯片推迟到2023年后，台积电股价当天涨了10%，市值一度超过特斯拉、英特尔和宝洁的总和，还宣布3纳米芯片将于2022年下半年量产。台积电每年的芯片产量以数十亿计，英特尔则以百万计——量级上差了好几个档次。

Intel's Past Failures  /  英特尔的过往失误

Although Intel remained the preferred partner for laptops and PC makers, it was not the preferred chip supplier for smartphone and mobile device companies. When smartphone sales took off, phone manufacturers turned to companies like Qualcomm Inc. and TSMC. When Intel ventured into chips for mobile phones, it did not allocate its best production and design to this category, as the PC and server chip businesses remained its preferred areas. Intel's 4G modems were slower than Qualcomm's. Intel was also lagging in 5G technical expertise compared to Qualcomm.

英特尔在笔记本和PC领域依然是首选合作伙伴，但在智能手机和移动设备领域完全不是首选。手机市场爆发时，手机厂商转向了高通和台积电。英特尔虽然尝试进军手机芯片，但始终没把最好的资源投进去，PC和服务器才是它的亲儿子。英特尔的4G基带就是跑不过高通，在5G技术储备上也落后于高通。

Technology continued progressing for AI and autonomous vehicles, but Intel did not invest much in these categories. In 2018, 85 per cent of its revenues came from servers and PCs. Intel even tried to reposition to make itself more suitable for such devices. Still, these efforts were not convincing to potential clients, as they believed that Intel lacked focus on designs for these devices.

AI和自动驾驶技术不断推进，但英特尔在这些赛道的投入一直不够。2018年，英特尔85%的营收仍然来自服务器和PC。英特尔也尝试过重新定位，但潜在客户并不买账，因为大家觉得英特尔在这些设备的芯片设计上根本没有专注投入。

François Piednoël, former principal engineer at Intel, said, "Intel is really out of focus." He further added that Intel's technical decisions since 2016 had been mostly "nonsense." He criticized Intel for building a culture where those employees with MBA degrees were promoted over those who had technical prowess, resulting in "no innovation, no aggressive road-maps, and no people driven because they are discouraged because the MBAs are the only ones rising."

英特尔前首席工程师弗朗索瓦·皮耶德诺埃尔毫不客气："英特尔真的太散了，没有焦点。"他还说2016年以来英特尔的技术决策大多是"胡说八道"，并批评英特尔的晋升文化偏向MBA背景的人，导致"没有创新，没有进取心的产品路线图，工程师们士气低落，因为上升通道被MBA们堵死了"。他在英特尔工作了20年后于2017年离职。

Intel's Customers  /  英特尔的客户

Computer makers and data support centres of nuclear power stations, spacecraft, jets, and intelligence bureaus were using Intel's Xeon chips. In 2020, Hewlett Packard Enterprise Co., Dell Technologies Inc., and Lenovo Group Ltd. contributed 17 per cent, 15 per cent, and 12 per cent of Intel's net revenue, respectively.

电脑厂商、核电站、航天器、喷气机和情报机构的数据中心都在用英特尔的至强（Xeon）芯片。2020年，慧普企业、戴尔和联想分别贡献了英特尔17%、15%和12%的净营收。

Several key customers, including Aurora Computers and the US Department of Energy, were adversely impacted due to delays in Intel's chip manufacturing. In 2020, Intel received a further setback after Apple, one of its key customers, announced it would use processor chips of its own design for its Mac computers, to be manufactured by TSMC. This was happening in a year that was witnessing the highest demand for laptops and PCs in the entire decade due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

超算开发商Aurora Computers和美国能源部等重要客户都受到了英特尔制造延误的直接冲击。2020年，苹果宣布Mac电脑将改用自主设计、由台积电制造的处理器，这对英特尔又是一记重击。而这一切发生在PC需求十年来最旺盛的一年——新冠疫情把PC需求推到了前所未有的高点。

HR Issues  /  人才流失

Several key executives at Intel had resigned since early 2020. In March 2020, Naveen Rao, Intel's top AI executive, left. In May 2020, Craig Barratt, who was in charge of Intel's Connectivity Group, resigned. Top Intel chip design executive Jim Keller resigned in June 2020. In July 2020, Intel also announced the departure of Renduchintala, who Intel had initially poached from Qualcomm in February 2016.

2020年初以来，英特尔多名核心高管相继离职。3月，AI业务负责人纳文·拉奥离职；5月，负责互联互通业务的克雷格·巴拉特辞职；6月，顶级芯片设计高管吉姆·凯勒辞职；7月，当初从高通挖来的首席工程官伦杜钦塔拉也宣布离职。

According to the investor community, Intel's problem lay with the board of directors, which had not made the right CEO selection decision. One of the investors said, "Intel's board is the problem, they put a finance person as Chairman in the past, he retired, now they put another finance person as CEO."

投资人群体认为，英特尔的问题根子在董事会，一直没选对CEO。有投资者评论道："英特尔的董事会才是问题所在，以前搞财务的当董事长，退休了，又搞财务的来当CEO。"

The Road Ahead  /  前路何方

Ian Cutress, a senior CPU editor for AnandTech, said, "If Intel fails to execute properly, then it leaves a large hole for its main competition to step into, as well as eroding 20 years of confidence in Intel's ability to deliver high-performance and high-efficiency computing products."

AnandTech的资深CPU编辑伊恩·卡特雷斯说："如果英特尔再执行不到位，就不只是给竞争对手让出空间，更是在摧毁20年来外界对英特尔交付高性能、高效能计算产品能力的信心。"

Chang I-Chien, a tech analyst, predicting Intel's outsourcing strategy, stated, "It's even likely that in the longer term Intel could gradually reduce its chip manufacturing footprints and continue to outsource if it later proves that would be a more cost-efficient way." One of the experts said, "If the future is the cloud and AI, Intel needs a strong leader to aggressively guide it there."

台新证券的科技分析师张一健预测："从长远来看，如果事实证明外包更省钱，英特尔很可能会逐步收缩自己的制造版图，持续扩大外包规模。"有专家直言："如果未来属于云和AI，英特尔需要一个强有力的领导者，带着它冲进去。"

Should Intel's current CEO, Gelsinger, outsource the manufacturing of chips to resolve Intel's manufacturing crisis? In the long run, should it steer its focus in a direction similar to those of its competitors, who were capturing new opportunities in cloud computing, drone, and AI technologies?

盖尔辛格到底该不该用外包来解决英特尔的制造危机？从长远来看，英特尔是否应该像竞争对手那样，把战略重心转向云计算、无人机和AI等新兴技术？
